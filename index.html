Designing and building a 3D Printer for food

The full prototype assembled at the end of the semester. Features a control panel to select the print file and control settings such as print speed. Food (edible fluid) is inserted into the container at the top of the printer.

The wiring diagram showing the motors, motor drivers, endstops to control axis position, and LCD control panel. This panel attaches to the Arduino controller and can accomodate the printer components which need a higher power supply.

Delta motion close-up. Each axis moves along a lead screw. An endstop is attached at the top of each axis to calibrate the position. The motors are attached under the couplers at the bottom of each axis.
